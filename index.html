Students have been given access to the Straits Times articles to encourage them to cultivate news reading habits and be engaged with current affairs and national issues.

This website is used to provide administrative information on logging into the ST app and also to share some best practices on encouraging the students to use the ST app.

Download the Straits Times Mobile App from Google Play Store or Apple App Store.

  1. Sign in using the following credential:
  • Username: your email address
  • Password: Use the default password that has been emailed to you

  1. Change the default password after login.
  • Click on the "Forgot your password?" link to change password.
  • A link to reset your password will be sent to your email account.

Caution: It is important to change the default password if you do not want unauthorized access to your account.

Open a web browser, and go to the following url: https://www.straitstimes.com/. Click on the "LOG IN" link at the top of the webpage.

  1. Sign in using the following credential:
  • Username: your email address
  • Password: Use the default password that has been emailed to you
  1. Change the default password after login.
  • Click on the "Forgot your password?" link to change password.
  • A link to reset your password will be sent to your email account.
  1. Change the default password (Alternative Approach)
  • Click on your account name at the top of the webpage after you have logged in. You can update your password using the pop-up dialog.

Caution: It is important to change the default password if you do not want unauthorized access to your account.

SPH allows lecturers to download and save premium articles for sharing with other lecturers to enable them to better prepare for class. However, these downloaded articles should not be shared with the public via social media or messaging apps.

Follow the steps given below for capturing and saving articles as pdf file:

  1. Open Chrome browser and navigate to the extension at

https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/full-page-screen-capture/fdpohaocaechififmbbbbbknoalclacl

  1. Install the chrome extension
  1. Click the capture button for the Straits Times article that you would like to take a screen screenshot.
  1. Click on the "pdf" button to save the article as a pdf file.
